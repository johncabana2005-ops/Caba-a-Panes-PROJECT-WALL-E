The WALL-E robot is an autonomous waste collection robot designed to help keep the environment clean by collecting and processing waste materials with minimal human 
assistance. The robot moves around a designated area while continuously scanning its surroundings using ultrasonic sensors. These sensors allow it to detect both waste 
items and obstacles, helping it navigate safely and efficiently.

When the robot detects a piece of waste, it moves toward the object and positions itself for collection. A robotic arm then picks up the waste and places it into the 
robot's processing compartment. Once collected, the system identifies the type of material, such as plastic, paper, or other lightweight waste, before sending it to a 
crushing mechanism. Crushing the waste reduces its size, allowing the robot to store more collected materials and improve its overall efficiency.

After the waste has been processed, the robot records the collection in its system. This information can be used to monitor the robot's performance, track the amount of 
waste collected, and evaluate the effectiveness of the waste management process. The robot then continues its operation by searching for additional waste within its 
assigned area.

At the center of the system is an Arduino microcontroller, which controls the robot's movement, sensors, robotic arm, and crushing mechanism. The Arduino serves as the 
robot's brain, ensuring that all components work together smoothly. To make the system more sustainable, the robot is powered by solar energy during daytime operation. 
Solar panels convert sunlight into electrical energy, providing a renewable power source that reduces electricity consumption and supports environmentally friendly 
operation.

Overall, the WALL-E robot combines automation, renewable energy, and waste management technologies into a single system. By reducing the need for manual waste collection 
and promoting cleaner surroundings, the robot can be a useful solution for schools, parks, communities, and other public areas. It also demonstrates how modern technology 
can be applied to address environmental challenges and support sustainable development.
